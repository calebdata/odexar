Why Most Final-Year Students Start Their Research in the Wrong Place

A significant proportion of final-year students have their research topics rejected at the first or second submission. In many cases, supervisors end up rewriting the topic entirely. While this may allow the student to proceed, it often produces work that lacks ownership and depth.

The underlying problem is not a lack of effort. It is a lack of thinking structure. Students are trained to think in terms of topics, not problems.
