How to Choose a Strong Research Topic

Choosing a research topic is one of the earliest and most consequential decisions in any academic project. Many students rush through it, picking something that sounds interesting without testing whether it is actually researchable or narrow enough for their word count and timeline.

A strong topic is specific, researchable, and aligned with your academic context. It is not just a subject area — it is a focused question you can investigate within your scope. If you cannot find at least ten credible sources on your topic within an hour, that is a signal to refine it further.
